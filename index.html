About IndabaX Sudan

IIndabaX Sudan is a locally organized chapter of the Deep Learning Indaba movement, dedicated to celebrating and strengthening the Sudanese AI and machine learning community. As part of the wider IndabaX network across Africa, our goal is to spread knowledge, build capacity, and create welcoming spaces where students, researchers, engineers, and industry professionals can learn together and collaborate on impactful ideas. We believe that nurturing local talent and fostering connections across disciplines is key to ensuring Sudan is an active shaper of AI’s future on the continent.

Each year, IndabaX Sudan curates talks from regional and international experts, hands-on workshops, mentorship, and community showcases that spotlight Sudan-relevant applications, from healthcare and agriculture to fintech, education, and climate resilience. By aligning with the Deep Learning Indaba’s mission of strengthening African AI, we provide practical skills, research exposure, and networks that help attendees turn curiosity into capability and ideas into action, whether you’re just getting started or pushing the frontier of ML research.

Testimonials

Hear from past IndabaX Sudan participants about how the event inspired and shaped their journeys in AI and data science.

Almonzer Hamid

ML Researcher and data science passionate

The sessions opened my eyes to the real-world applications of AI in Africa, and the hands-on workshops helped me strengthen my technical and problem-solving skills. Networking with talented researchers and professionals also expanded my perspective and encouraged me to pursue AI more seriously as a career path. Overall, it was a transformative experience that boosted both my confidence and curiosity in the field.

Tsabeeh Hamed

Data Scientist | Data Analyst

I attended IndabaX Sudan 2021, and it was a turning point in my data science journey. The conference introduced me to real world AI applications and inspired me to strengthen my technical and analytical skills. Through workshops and discussions with local and international experts, I gained valuable knowledge about machine learning, data visualization, and ethical AI. Meeting professionals and students who shared the same passion for data motivated me to keep learning and collaborate on meaningful projects. The event expanded my professional network and gave me a clearer vision of how AI can drive development in Sudan.

Alaa Almouiz F. Moh.

ML Research | LLMs | Dual Degrees: EEE @ U of K & Robotics, Autonomous and Interactive Systems @ Heriot-Watt University | Equitech Scholar ESP’23.

A defining moment in my trajectory was when I attended the IndabaXSudan in 2019 which was my first exposure to ML, with sessions taught in my own Sudanese language. In 2022, I was selected with 350 Africans for a one week Deep Learning Indaba in Tunisia to move from as a national attendee to an international attendee. A session by Masakhane on NLP motivated my interest in ML Ressearch for my distteration two years later. After my return from Indaba, I was nomintaed by IndabaXSudan’s main organizer, Ahmed AlHassan, to be the Media Coordinator for that year’s Indaba. We amplified the voice of ML across Sudan to have 235 attendees with different expertise in the field. Having first discovered ML through Indaba, I feel responsible for passing the influence to others through our local language. This year, I'm leading the IndabaXSudan family, and despite what’s happening in Sudan, we decided to spread ML beyond borders for our Sudanese community despite the ongoing situation.

Safia Gibril N.Abdalla

ML researcher | Data science @MITEmergingTalent

IndabaX Sudan was the spark that fueled my passion for Machine Learning. I was deeply inspired by IndabaX’s supportive community. Then what started as a curiosity turned into a lasting commitment to learning ML and giving back to this community, taking on various roles before eventually leading the event in 2025. Through the journey I saw how knowledge sharing through Indaba empowered us as Africans especially when I attended a faculty panel on DLI 2025 on how Africans can take ownership of solutions to their own challenges. The inspiration from both national and international speakers awakened my desire to deepen my understanding of the field. It ultimately motivated me to pursue research on the evaluation of Large Language Models (LLMs).
